Holding the Chat

Keeping live chat enquiries warm and moving

💬

Our FAQs send customers to chat. Once they are talking to you, the job is to keep them moving forward, never let them hit a brick wall and quietly drift off.

The Core Mindset
🎯
Every reply either moves them forward or captures them for follow-up. Never just a dead-end.

A missing date, a price that is not out yet, a vague enquiry; none of these are reasons to stall the chat or send the customer away. They are moments to keep shaping the trip, give an honest steer, and own the next step. A held lead is a future booking. An abandoned chat books with someone else.

Challenge 1  ·  The Date Trap
📅
The quote system needs a date. The customer often does not have one yet.

Get a workable date without making the customer feel cornered or rushed off the chat. The date is for our system; it is not a commitment from them. Treat it that way and they will give it freely.

✨ Lead with the trip, not the date
  • Do not open with "What are your dates?" It reads like a form.
  • Get them talking about where and what first; the date comes easily once they are invested.
Try"Tell me what you're picturing and I'll start shaping some options around it."
🧭 Offer easy anchors
  • Replace the blank "when?" with simple choices.
  • Narrow gently: this year or next, then season, then a month.
Try"Are we thinking this year, or planning ahead for next? And more a school-holiday trip or a quieter time?"
📋 Use a working date, openly
  • Tell them you will pop in a placeholder so the system can run.
  • This keeps the quote moving and the customer relaxed.
Try"Pricing shifts a lot by season, so let me use early [month] as a starting point; we'll move it the moment you decide."
💰 Make flexibility pay
  • If dates can flex even a few days, you can often find better value.
  • Turns the date question into a benefit for them.
Try"If you're flexible, I can compare a couple of windows and show you where the best value sits."
💡 Always say why
  • People give a date readily once they understand the reason.
  • Frame it as protecting them from numbers that change later.
Try"I just need a rough timeframe, prices and availability move with the season, so this keeps the numbers honest for you."
🎯 For the truly undecided
  • Peg the date to something real: a season, an event, a window.
  • Anything concrete is easier to answer than an open diary.
Try"Are we tying this to anything; a birthday, school break, an anniversary? Or roughly within the next six months?"
⚠️

On placeholder dates: a working date is fine to pull an indicative quote, but flag it clearly as "indicative, dates to be confirmed" so the customer never treats a placeholder figure as locked-in pricing.

Challenge 2  ·  Read the Traveller
🧠
The Know Your Customer guide covers what to ask. This is who you are talking to.

Spot the persona early and you already know what to lock in and how to pitch, without firing off a list of questions. Read the chat, hook into what they have shared, and let one or two natural questions confirm the rest.

💍 The Romance Trip
Spot them by
  • "Honeymoon", "just engaged", "our anniversary", a couple, milestone language
Lock in
  • Privacy, special touches, room category
  • Beach-only or a safari-and-beach combo
💍 Easiest place to add value with genuine little touches
👪 The Family Holiday
Spot them by
  • Kids mentioned, "school holidays", lots of value questions
Lock in
  • Dates are tied to school terms; get the kids' ages early
  • Family rooms, kids' club, direct flights; all-inclusive often wins
👶 The kids' ages decide the property
🎂 The Milestone / Bucket-list
Spot them by
  • "Always wanted to", a big birthday, retirement, "once in a lifetime"
Lock in
  • The experience matters more than shaving cost
  • Often willing to stretch for the right trip
🎉 Getting it right beats the lowest price
🧹 The First-timer
Spot them by
  • Lots of "how much", hesitation, visa and logistics questions
Lock in
  • Clear inclusions and no hidden surprises
  • Hand-holding on visas, transfers and what to expect
💬 Confidence closes this customer, not a discount
🚢 The Repeat Traveller
Spot them by
  • References to past trips, line or brand loyalty, firm preferences
Lock in
  • What worked last time; the specific line or region
  • Treat them as a regular and recognise the loyalty
⭐ Don't re-explain what they already know
👯 The Group Getaway
Spot them by
  • "A group of us", "a few couples", "my friends and I"
Lock in
  • Who decides and who pays; one point of contact
  • Room configs, shared versus separate arrangements
🗣️ Find the organiser and make them look good
🔗

Pairs with the Know Your Customer guide: use the persona to set your angle, then lean on the Six Profile Pillars for the detail questions; who's travelling, the occasion, budget mindset, travel style and upsell signals.

Challenge 3  ·  No Rates Out Yet
⏳
"Check back closer to the time" loses the customer.

When this year's rates are not released, hold them three ways: give an honest indication, sell the early-bird advantage, and own the follow-up with a real date. The goal is that the customer never has to chase us, and never has a reason to go shopping elsewhere while they wait.

🚫 Never end on "come back later"
  • Replace the dead-end with a capture and a promise.
  • Make the wait feel like an advantage they are signing up for.
Try"Let me take your details and I'll be first to send you exact pricing the moment new rates land, often before they go public."
💲 Give an honest indication
  • Offer a steer based on real last-season figures, clearly flagged.
  • Never quote a firm number you do not have.
Try"New-season rates aren't out yet. To give you a feel, a similar trip recently ran in the region of [last-season range]; new pricing usually lands close to that."
🕒 Sell the early-bird advantage
  • Booking ahead often locks the best rate and the rooms people actually want.
  • Frame the wait as the advantage, not the obstacle.
Try"Booking early usually means better rates and first pick of rooms; I'd hate for you to miss out by waiting."
📊 Offer a priced option now
  • Give them a real benchmark with live pricing for a comparable period.
  • Keeps momentum instead of leaving the chat empty-handed.
Try"I can pull live pricing for a similar period right now, so you've got real numbers to work from while we wait on the new rates."
🗓️ Own the follow-up with a date
  • Do not make the customer chase you.
  • Commit to a specific date you will actually hit.
Try"New rates are expected around [month]. Can I set a reminder and come back to you on [date] with exact numbers?"
✨ Keep building the dream
  • Do not let price be the only thread holding the chat together.
  • Talk inclusions, the property, the experience; keep them excited.
Try"While we wait on rates, let me tell you what makes this one special, I think it's exactly what you're after."
⚠️

Never invent a price. Any indicative figure must be a real last-season number, clearly labelled "indicative, subject to new rates". A made-up number you cannot honour does more damage than giving no number at all.

Challenge 4  ·  Buying Time to Quote
⏱️
You don't have to leave the chat to quote, and you don't have to quote on the spot either.

Building a quote means jumping to the quote system, which closes the screen the customer is on, and not everyone has a second screen yet. If you have time to build it live, do it. If not, buy yourself a little time, capture the customer, and send it through shortly after. Chat is a rostered slot of about 1.5 hours, so the customer is never waiting forever; just set a realistic time and honour it.

🧐 Read whether they'll wait
  • If they are keen and have a few minutes, build it live.
  • If they are busy, don't hold them hostage in the chat; take details and send it on.
Try"Happy to build this with you now if you've got a few minutes, or I can send it through shortly, whichever suits you better?"
✨ Set the expectation upfront
  • A proper quote is crafted, not a number off the top of your head.
  • Position the short wait as tailoring, not a delay.
Try"I'll put together a proper tailored quote rather than a rough figure; give me a little time to get it exactly right for you."
📋 Capture everything first
  • Before you step away, confirm every detail so you don't have to come back with more questions.
  • Who's travelling, the working date, key preferences, and the best contact.
Try"Before I work on this, let me just confirm a couple of things so I can get it spot on in one go."
📧 Move it to email or WhatsApp
  • The chat window doesn't have to be the only thread.
  • Get a contact so the conversation continues after they close the screen.
Try"What's the best email or WhatsApp for the full quote? That way you've got it to keep and compare in your own time."
🕛 Give a time, then beat it
  • Commit to a clear window that fits around your chat roster, then aim to send earlier.
  • Underpromise, overdeliver; a realistic time you hit beats a fast one you miss.
Try"I'll have this over to you by [time] today." or "I'll get this to you first thing tomorrow."
💬 Don't go silent
  • Dead air loses customers; a quick holding message keeps momentum while you work.
  • Even a one-line "still on it" keeps them warm.
Try"Great, I'm on it now, pulling your options together; bear with me a few minutes."
⚠️

Honour the time you commit to. Because chat is a rostered 1.5-hour slot, you may not be back on chat when the quote is ready, so set the follow-up to something you can deliver by email or WhatsApp, then deliver it. A missed callback does more damage than a slightly longer estimate.

Say This. Not That.
✅  Open Door
  • "Give me a rough month and I'll pull indicative pricing; we firm it up later."
  • "Are we thinking this year, or planning ahead?"
  • "I'll be first to send you exact pricing the second new rates land."
  • "Let me set a reminder and come back to you on [date]."
  • "Sounds like a family trip; how old are the kids, so I match the right resort?"
  • "Are you after best value, comfortable mid-range, or spoil-yourselves?"
  • "If your dates can flex, I can often find better value."
  • "I'll build you a proper quote and send it by [time]; best email or WhatsApp for it?"
  • "Happy to do this with you now, or send it shortly, whichever suits you?"
❌  Brick Wall
  • "I can't quote without a date." (and stopping there)
  • "What are your dates?" as the very first message
  • "Those rates aren't out yet." (and stopping there)
  • "Come back closer to the time."
  • "How many people, what budget, what dates?" all at once
  • "What's your budget?" as a blunt opener
  • Quoting a firm price you are really just guessing at
  • Saying "hold on" and then going silent while you switch screens
  • Promising "two minutes" and then taking an hour
Why This Matters
🚫
Fewer Dead-Ends
Every chat ends with a next step or a captured lead, not a quiet goodbye and a customer who drifts off.
📈
More Conversions
Held customers come back and book. Abandoned chats book with whoever kept the conversation going instead.
🤝
Trust
Honest indications and owned follow-ups make Thompsons For Travel the agent they choose to come back to.
📝

The one rule: whatever the question, never let the chat dead-end. If you cannot answer fully right now, capture the customer and own the next step with a date you will actually hit. Note what you learned so the follow-up feels like a continuation, not a restart. A held lead is a future booking; an abandoned chat is a competitor's.
